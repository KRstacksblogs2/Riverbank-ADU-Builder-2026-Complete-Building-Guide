What Is an ADU and Why Is Riverbank the Right Place to Build One?

An Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU) is a self-contained residential unit — with its own kitchen, bathroom, and sleeping area — located on the same lot as a primary home. In Riverbank, ADUs can be detached structures, garage conversions, additions attached to your home, or Junior ADUs carved out of existing interior space.

Riverbank ADU Permitting: What You Need to Know in 2026

Step 1: Confirm Zoning Eligibility

Riverbank ADUs are permitted on single-family and multi-family residential lots throughout the city. Use the Stanislaus County Interactive Permit Guide (available at stancounty.com) to enter your address and verify your parcel's ADU eligibility, required setbacks, and applicable zoning standards before you begin design.

Step 2: Choose Your ADU Plan

Riverbank property owners have access to seven pre-approved ADU floor plans developed jointly by Stanislaus County and the City of Riverbank through TPH Architects. Step 3: Submit Your Permit Application

Building permit applications for Riverbank ADU projects can be submitted online through the Stanislaus County Accela Citizen Access portal, or in person at 1010 10th Street, Suite 3400. Under California state law (updated through 2026), the permitting agency must respond to your complete application within 60 days — or the permit is deemed approved by law.

Step 4: Construction, Inspections & Final Occupancy

Once your permit is issued, construction can begin. Pinnacle Building & Restoration manages the full build and coordinates all required inspections — foundation, framing, electrical, plumbing, mechanical, and final — ensuring a smooth path to your Certificate of Occupancy.

2026 California ADU Laws: Key Changes That Benefit Riverbank Owners

California's ADU law landscape continues to evolve in favor of property owners. Here are the most important 2026 changes affecting Riverbank ADU projects:

  • No Owner-Occupancy Required (AB 976) — Permanently removes the requirement that you live on the property to build or rent an ADU. Riverbank investors can now rent both the primary home and the ADU freely.
  • 60-Day Permit Approval or Deemed Approved — If Riverbank's permitting agency doesn't act on a complete ADU application within 60 days, it is automatically deemed approved by state law.
  • No Impact Fees on ADUs Under 750 Sq Ft — ADUs under 750 square feet are fully exempt from construction, connection, school, and impact fees — significantly reducing project costs for smaller units.
  • Unpermitted ADU Legalization (AB 2533) — Homeowners with unpermitted ADUs built before January 1, 2020 can now pursue legalization. Agencies must provide a clear path to compliance.
  • Up to 8 ADUs on Multi-Family Lots (SB 1211) — Multi-family property owners in Riverbank can now build up to 8 detached ADUs on eligible parcels — a major opportunity for investors.
  • Free Pre-Approved Plans Required Statewide — All California municipalities are now required to have pre-approved ADU plan options available. Riverbank already has 7 free plans ready to use.
